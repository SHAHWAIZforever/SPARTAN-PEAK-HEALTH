BMI Categories:


Underweight: BMI < 18.5
Normal weight: 18.5 ≤ BMI < 24.9
Overweight: 25 ≤ BMI < 29.9
Obesity Class 1 (Moderate): 30 ≤ BMI < 34.9
Obesity Class 2 (Severe): 35 ≤ BMI < 39.9
Obesity Class 3 (Very severe or morbidly obese): BMI ≥ 40
These categories help assess whether an individual is underweight, healthy, overweight, or obese based on their BMI value.

MAINTAINANCE CALORIES CALCULATOR

Maintainance Calories:

NOTE: Your Age should be 18+. Otherwise result will not be considered right.


Maintenance calories refer to the number of calories your body needs to consume in a day to maintain your current weight, without gaining or losing. Achieving a balance between calories consumed and calories burned is key to maintaining weight. Age actually doesn't matter much when we calculate MAINTAINANCE CALORIES because weight itself needs calories. As you get older your activity level can decrease so adjust accordingly.



Sedentary Lifestyle

Description: Little to no physical activity. Most of the time spent sitting or lying down.

Examples:

  • Sitting at a desk for work or school all day.
  • Watching TV for long periods.
  • Reading or using a computer for hours without physical movement.

Lightly Active Lifestyle

Description: Some light physical activity, but still a majority of the day spent inactive or with minimal movement.

Examples:

  • Light walking (e.g., walking around the house or office).
  • Short strolls or errands.
  • Casual cycling or recreational sports once or twice a week.
  • Housewives: Performing household chores like light cleaning, cooking, and organizing.

Moderately Active Lifestyle

Description: Regular exercise, moderate-intensity activities, or more frequent movement throughout the day.

Examples:

  • Regular walks, jogging, or light fitness routines.
  • Participating in moderate-intensity exercise 3-5 times a week.
  • HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training) workouts or going to the gym regularly.
  • Daily movement, like walking to work or taking the stairs.

Very Active Lifestyle

Description: High levels of physical activity, often involving rigorous exercise or manual labor.

Examples:

  • Intense workouts like running, swimming, or cycling 6-7 days a week.
  • Participating in competitive sports or physically demanding jobs (e.g., construction work).
  • Weight training or heavy lifting sessions.
  • Athletes: Professional athletes training several hours a day, including sports practice and physical conditioning.

Extremely Active Lifestyle

Description: physical activity, such as daily exercise, athletic training, or physical labor.

Examples:

  • Professional athletes training for Few hours a day.
  • Bodybuilding Athlete,Tenis Player
  • Jobs that require physical exertion,Construction Worker

ATHLETE

Description: Peak physical activity, such as intense daily exercise, rigorous athletic training, or extreme physical labor.

Examples:

  • Professional athletes training for several hours a day.
  • CrossFit, endurance training, or high-intensity interval training (HIIT) multiple times per week.
  • Jobs that require intense physical exertion, like firefighting or military service.
